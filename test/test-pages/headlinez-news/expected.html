A complex international operation successfully airlifted 147 elite show jumping horses from Qatar to Liège, Belgium, after competitions in the region were canceled due to escalating tensions surrounding Iran. The massive relocation effort, completed in just 36 hours, was a joint undertaking by the Doha Equestrian Tour, Qatari authorities, and Qatar Airways.

The horses, representing numerous nations and among the world’s top competitors, were initially slated to participate in events in Qatar. However, the competitions were called off amid growing geopolitical concerns. With air travel disrupted by the crisis, a unique logistical challenge emerged to acquire the animals safely home.

Unable to fly directly from Doha, the horses first embarked on a 350-kilometer road journey to Riyadh, the capital of Saudi Arabia. From there, Qatar Airways Cargo chartered Boeing 777F aircraft to transport the horses to Liège, Belgium – a major European hub for live animal freight. Two flights were required, carrying 74 and 73 horses respectively, along with their caretakers, veterinarians, and essential supplies.

Comfort and Safety Ensured During Transport

Qatar Airways has extensive experience in transporting horses and other animals globally, and the operation was meticulously planned to minimize stress. The aircraft were fitted with specially designed “jet stalls” – individual, padded, and climate-controlled boxes within the cargo hold – providing ample space and a comfortable environment for the six-hour flight.

“We express our deep gratitude to all the official partners and authorities involved in the safe and successful transport of the horses,” a representative from the Doha Equestrian Tour stated. The smooth execution of the operation was also welcomed by competitors, including German rider Janne Friederike Meyer‑Zimmermann, who expressed her relief on social media once the aircraft landed safely in Belgium. “Our horses are on their way home safely. Thank you, Doha Tour!” she wrote.

The successful repatriation of these horses highlights the significant impact of international events on the equestrian world and the dedication required to ensure the well-being of these athletes when faced with unforeseen circumstances.
